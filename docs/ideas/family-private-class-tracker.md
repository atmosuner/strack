# Family Private Class Tracker — Refined Concept

## Problem Statement

**How might we** help US households with **two or more private lessons per week** (sports and tutoring) keep **time, place, provider, and money status** aligned—without relying on scattered chats and generic calendars—so that **some families pay early** because the alternative is clearly more expensive (money or stress)?

## Recommended Direction

Ship a **household-first, lesson-native calendar** as the core experience: every block is a **lesson** with **child**, **provider**, **location**, and **recurrence**, plus **light payment/package state** so "what's owed / what's left" lives next to the schedule. Use **ICS subscribe/export** early to reduce "another silo" friction.

Because your **90-day success metric is a real money signal**, pair product work with **deliberate monetization learning**: **paid beta, refundable deposit, or annual prep** for early access—not "free until we feel like charging." Keep the **wedge narrow** (private lessons, not whole family OS) so messaging and onboarding stay sharp.

## Key Assumptions to Validate

- [ ] **Habit:** Target households will **keep** a dedicated app if calendar export works and onboarding is under 10 minutes. *Test:* cohort retention + ICS usage.
- [ ] **WTP:** They'll **pay** for lesson coordination + money clarity, not only use it free. *Test:* deposit or paid beta; Van Westendorp on annual price.
- [ ] **Pain ranking:** Scheduling chaos vs package/credit confusion—which **pulls** harder for pay? *Test:* forced-rank in interviews + concierge notes.
- [ ] **Second caregiver:** Invites increase retention and willingness to pay. *Test:* invite rate by day 7 among payers vs non-payers.

## MVP Scope

**In:** household + children + providers; lesson CRUD + recurrence (weekly v1 acceptable); agenda/week + "today/this week"; **ICS**; **basic roles**; **paid/unpaid + simple credits** (define minimal rules); reminders.

**Near after first paid cohort:** tighten money UX, attachments, stronger sync—**only** if retention/export metrics justify.

## Not Doing (and Why)

- **Marketplace to find coaches** — different business; distracts from household workflow.
- **Full school administration** — wrong buyer and sales motion.
- **Perfect two-way Google sync in v1** — high cost; **ICS first** to validate habit and pay.
- **Social network for parents** — no pull for your metric; privacy cost.
- **Global launch before US beachhead works** — splits focus and compliance.
